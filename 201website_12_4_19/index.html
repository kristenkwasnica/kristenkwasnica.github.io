Photoshop: Color Your World

For this project I was assigned to change the color of two items and I wanted to specifically focus on the clothing. I created different layers for the different skirt edits and used the quick selection tool to grab cover the entirety of each skirt. Then I made a hue/saturation mask on the adjustment layer and changed hue and saturation settings to replace the colors of the skirts.

Photoshop: Skin Retouching

For each face in this photoshop series, I first used the patch tool to remove the large, noticeable blemishes. I used the patch tool to draw a circle around the blemishes and then used the cursor over the selected area and dragged to an area that I wanted to use as my source. In this case, an area without blemishes. I repeated this throughout the entire face. Next, I wanted to create a more even texture throughout the entirety of the face and I used the clone stamp tool. With the clone stamp tool, first you option-click to define the source, and option-control then drag cursor right to create bigger surface area of clone stamp. Then you simply position the cursor over the area where you want to paint the cloned pixels and then start painting by clicking on the area and repeat until satisfied. For the man, I used the patch tool and the clone stamp tool to clean up some of the eyebrow hairs and facial hair. For the last two pictures I liquified their faces by clicking filter then liquify to enhance certain facial features.
